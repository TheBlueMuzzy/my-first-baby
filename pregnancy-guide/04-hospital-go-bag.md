# Hospital Go-Bag

Pack by ~wk 35-36 and keep it by the door or in the car. Use two bags: a small "labor" bag and a larger "after" bag. Stays are short (~24-48 hrs vaginal, ~2-4 days C-section), so do not overpack.

The hospital usually provides (do not duplicate): diapers, wipes, pads, mesh underwear, peri bottle, baby blankets, and sometimes formula. Confirm with yours.

## Mom
- Going-home outfit, loose and comfortable (still looks ~6 months pregnant).
- Robe, nightgown or labor gown (front-opening if nursing), non-slip socks, slippers.
- Comfortable underwear you do not mind ruining; nursing bras + pads.
- Toiletries: toothbrush, deodorant, dry shampoo, hair ties, lip balm, glasses/contacts.
- Phone + extra-long charging cable.
- Own pillow with a distinct (non-white) pillowcase so it does not get lost.
- Snacks and drinks for after delivery.
- Optional: eye mask, earplugs, preferred postpartum pads.

## Baby
- Car seat, already installed and inspected (lives in the car, not the bag).
- Going-home outfit + one backup in a different size.
- 2-3 onesies, socks, hat, mittens, swaddle blanket (weather-appropriate).

## Partner (dad)
- Change of clothes for 1-2+ days; comfortable layers (rooms run cold or hot).
- Toiletries, deodorant, toothbrush.
- Snacks + cash/card for vending and cafeteria.
- Phone + charger, headphones.
- Pillow and a blanket (you will likely sleep on a fold-out).
- Entertainment for long downtime stretches.

## Documents / admin
- Photo IDs (both of you).
- Insurance card.
- Hospital pre-registration confirmation.
- Birth plan, several printed copies.
- Chosen pediatrician's name + contact (required for discharge).
- Any relevant OB notes or medical records.

## Setup before labor (not in the bag)
- Car seat installed + inspected.
- Hospital route, backup route, parking, after-hours entrance mapped.
- Contact/call tree set; pet and childcare covered.

> Reference only. Confirm hospital-provided items with your facility.
