# Fourth Trimester (0-12 Weeks)

> VACCINE-SCHEDULE CAVEAT (as of 2026-06-29): The infant vaccine schedule is in flux. In Jan 2026 the CDC adopted a revised schedule developed outside the normal ACIP process; the AAP broke from it and published its own (endorsed by 12+ organizations); a federal court stayed the CDC 2026 schedule on Mar 16, 2026, reverting it to the May 2025 version, with an appeal filed Apr 29, 2026. Your pediatrician is the source of truth for exact timing. The well-visit cadence below is stable; treat the vaccine list as "typical," confirmed visit by visit.

## Pediatric visits (well-baby, stable cadence)
- Newborn check (~3-5 days after discharge).
- 1 month, 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, 9 months, 12 months.
- Each visit: weight/length/head measurements, feeding + development check, vaccines.

## Typical first-year vaccines (confirm with pediatrician)
- HepB: birth, then ~1-2 mo and 6-18 mo.
- 2, 4, 6 months: DTaP, Hib, polio (IPV), pneumococcal (PCV), rotavirus (oral).
- 12 months: MMR, varicella, Hep A, Hib + PCV boosters.
- Flu: yearly starting at 6 months.
- RSV: baby gets the nirsevimab antibody if mom did not get the RSV vaccine in pregnancy.

## Mom recovery
- Bleeding (lochia) for up to ~6 weeks; pads only, no tampons.
- Perineal or C-section incision care per discharge instructions.
- No heavy lifting beyond the baby; pelvic rest (no sex) until cleared.
- 6-week postpartum checkup (physical + mental health screen).
- CALL PROVIDER NOW for: soaking a pad in under an hour, fever, calf pain/swelling (clot), severe headache or vision changes (postpartum preeclampsia is real and can appear after birth).

## Newborn basics
- Feed on demand, ~8-12 times/day; pediatrician tracks weight + wet/dirty diapers.
- Vitamin D drops (400 IU/day) if breastfeeding.
- Umbilical cord: keep dry, sponge baths only until it falls off (~1-2 wks).
- ANY fever in a baby under 3 months (100.4 F / 38 C rectal) is an ER trip, immediately.
- Watch for jaundice (yellowing); flag at visits.

## Safe sleep (memorize: A-B-C)
- Alone, on Back, in a bare Crib.
- Firm flat surface, no blankets/pillows/bumpers/toys.
- Room-share without bed-sharing for ~6 months.

## Mental health (both parents)
- "Baby blues" common the first ~2 weeks.
- Postpartum depression/anxiety can start anytime in the first year and needs treatment, not waiting it out.
- Dads get postpartum depression too; do not dismiss it.

## Partner (dad) tasks
- Take night shifts, diapering, bottle feeds, burping; own the household + meals.
- Be the visitor gatekeeper (screen, schedule, enforce rest).
- Watch mom for the physical warning signs above and for PPD; push for help early.
- Take paternity leave if available; do skin-to-skin to bond.
- Support the feeding choice (breast/formula/both) without judgment.
- Handle admin: birth certificate, SSN, adding baby to insurance (tight deadlines).
