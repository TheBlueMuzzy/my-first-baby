# Labor Signs & When to Go In

Provider-specific thresholds override everything here. Get your exact "when to call/come in" numbers at a late-pregnancy visit and write them down.

## Early signs (can precede labor by days)
- Baby drops (lightening); easier breathing, more pelvic pressure.
- Loss of mucus plug / "bloody show" (pink-tinged discharge).
- Loose stools, nesting urge.
- Braxton Hicks getting stronger but staying irregular.

## Real labor vs. false labor
- Real: contractions get longer, stronger, closer together, and do not stop when you move or rest.
- False (Braxton Hicks): irregular, do not intensify, ease with movement or hydration.

## The common go-in rule (confirm yours): "5-1-1"
- Contractions 5 minutes apart.
- Each lasting ~1 minute.
- Sustained for 1 hour.
- First-time labors are often long, so many are told to labor at home until this point.

## Go to the hospital now (do not wait for 5-1-1) if:
- Water breaks: note T.A.C.O. to report: Time it happened, Amount, Color, Odor. Green/brown fluid or foul smell needs immediate attention.
- Bleeding heavier than spotting.
- Reduced or no fetal movement.
- Severe constant pain, or a contraction that never fully relaxes.
- Severe headache, vision changes, sudden swelling (preeclampsia).
- Contractions before ~37 weeks (possible preterm labor).
- Cord prolapse (visible cord) or feeling of something in the birth canal: call 911.

## Broad stages (timing varies widely)
- Stage 1: early + active labor + transition; cervix dilates to 10 cm (longest stage).
- Stage 2: pushing + birth (minutes to a few hours).
- Stage 3: delivering the placenta (~5-30 min).
- "Fourth stage": first 1-2 hrs of recovery + skin-to-skin.

## Partner (dad) tasks during labor
- Time contractions; know the go-threshold cold.
- Drive (or call the ride); bags and car seat ready at the door.
- Communicate with staff, track her stated preferences, advocate.
- Keep the call tree paused until she is ready to share.
- Hydration, ice chips, counter-pressure, calm presence.

> Reference only. Provider thresholds override. When in doubt, call.
