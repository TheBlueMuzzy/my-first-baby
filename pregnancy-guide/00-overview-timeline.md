# Overview & Timeline

Pregnancy is ~40 weeks, measured from the first day of the last menstrual period (LMP), across 3 trimesters, followed by the "fourth trimester" and the first year.

## The full timeline (high level)

### Pregnancy (~40 weeks, 3 trimesters)
- First trimester (wk 1-13): confirm pregnancy, first prenatal visit (~wk 8-10), early genetic screening options, highest miscarriage-risk window.
- Second trimester (wk 14-27): nausea usually eases, anatomy scan (~wk 18-22, often sex reveal), gestational diabetes screen starts (~wk 24-28).
- Third trimester (wk 28-40): Tdap vaccine (~wk 27-36), Group B strep test (~wk 36), go-bag ready, birth plan, frequent visits.
- Labor & birth (~wk 37-42): "full term" is 39-40; the due date is an estimate, not a deadline.

### First year (postpartum)
- Fourth trimester (0-12 wk): newborn survival mode, mom recovery, feeding established, 6-week postpartum checkup.
- Months 3-6: sleep/routine, solids prep (~6 mo), vaccine rounds.
- Months 6-12: mobility, solids, first words/steps, 12-month well visit.

## Key fixed checkpoints (the skeleton)
- First prenatal visit: ~wk 8-10
- NIPT / first-trimester screening: ~wk 10-13
- Anatomy scan: ~wk 18-22
- Glucose (gestational diabetes) test: ~wk 24-28
- Tdap shot (mom): ~wk 27-36
- Group B strep swab: ~wk 36
- Pediatric well-baby visits: birth, ~3-5 days, 1 mo, 2 mo, 4 mo, 6 mo, 9 mo, 12 mo

> Reference only. Your OB/midwife/pediatrician overrides this. Verify time-sensitive items (vaccines, food advisories) before relying on them.
