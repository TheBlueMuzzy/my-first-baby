# Months 3-12

## Months 3-6
- Pediatric visits at 4 and 6 months with vaccine rounds.
- Sleep: longer stretches possible; the "4-month sleep regression" is common and normal.
- Rolling over begins; never leave baby unattended on a raised surface.
- Drooling/teething may start; first tooth anywhere from ~4-12 months.
- Still breast milk/formula only until solids start.

## Starting solids (~6 months)
- Readiness signs: sits with support, good head control, interest in food, lost tongue-thrust reflex.
- Start single foods; introduce common allergens (peanut, egg) early and one at a time, which reduces allergy risk rather than increasing it.
- NEVER give honey before 12 months (infant botulism risk).
- No cow's milk as a main drink before 12 months (small amounts in food OK).
- Small amounts of water OK once on solids.
- Choking hazards to avoid: whole grapes, nuts, popcorn, hard raw veg, hot dog rounds, globs of nut butter.

## Months 6-12
- Pediatric visits at 9 and 12 months.
- Milestones (ranges, not deadlines): sitting, crawling, pulling to stand, possibly first steps + first words near 12 months.
- Object permanence brings separation anxiety and stranger anxiety.
- Naps consolidate; some families sleep-train in this window (multiple valid methods, pick one and stay consistent).

## Childproofing (do before mobility, ~6 months)
- Anchor furniture and TVs to walls (tip-over is a top injury).
- Outlet covers, cabinet/drawer latches, stair gates.
- Cords (blinds, electronics) out of reach.
- Lock away cleaning products, meds, small objects.
- Set water heater under 120 F; never leave baby alone near water.
- Get down to baby eye level and crawl the space to spot hazards.

## Partner (dad) tasks
- Split night wakings + early mornings fairly; agree on the sleep approach together.
- Lead childproofing and the car-seat transitions as baby grows.
- Do solo caregiving stretches so you are fully competent, not a "helper."
- Keep tracking both parents' mental load; rebalance openly.
- Manage milestone/medical records and the next round of admin (daycare start, etc.).

> Reference only. Pediatrician overrides on solids timing and milestones.
