# First Trimester (Weeks 1-13)

## Medical checkpoints
- First prenatal visit (~wk 8-10): dating ultrasound, full bloodwork (blood type/Rh, CBC, infection + immunity screen), urine test, medical history, confirm prenatal vitamin.
- Carrier screening (optional): blood test for inherited conditions; can be done now or preconception.
- NIPT / cell-free DNA (from ~wk 10): blood test screening for chromosomal conditions, can also reveal sex.
- NT scan + first-trimester screening (~wk 11-13): ultrasound measuring nuchal translucency.
- CVS (~wk 10-13): diagnostic test, only if screening flags risk, not routine.

## What's happening (mom)
- Nausea (any time of day), fatigue, sore breasts, frequent urination, food aversions, heightened smell.
- Highest miscarriage-risk window; many wait until ~wk 12-13 to announce.
- Folic acid matters most right now (neural tube forms early).

## Do
- Prenatal vitamin daily, 400-800 mcg folic acid.
- Choose OB or midwife; confirm hospital or birth center.
- Small frequent meals + hydration for nausea.
- Keep light exercise if already active (walking, swimming, prenatal yoga).

## Avoid (food)
- Alcohol: zero (no safe amount).
- Caffeine: under 200 mg/day (~one 12 oz coffee).
- Raw/undercooked meat, eggs, fish; raw sushi.
- High-mercury fish: shark, swordfish, king mackerel, marlin, orange roughy, bigeye tuna, tilefish; limit albacore to 6 oz/week.
- Unpasteurized dairy and soft cheeses (brie, feta, queso fresco) unless labeled pasteurized.
- Deli meat and hot dogs unless heated until steaming (listeria).
- Raw sprouts, unwashed produce, unpasteurized juice, liver/high-dose vitamin A.

## Avoid (activity/exposure)
- Smoking, vaping, recreational drugs.
- Hot tubs and saunas (overheating).
- Cat litter (toxoplasmosis).
- Harsh cleaning chemicals, solvents, pesticides.
- Contact sports, fall-risk activities, scuba diving.
- Any med/supplement (including OTC) not cleared by provider; non-essential X-rays.

## Partner (dad) tasks
- Take over cat litter, heavy lifting, harsh-chemical chores for now.
- Attend the first visit and dating scan if you can.
- Stock nausea-friendly food (crackers, ginger, bland snacks).
- Start a shared calendar + running question list for appointments.
- Respect her timeline on announcing; do not leak it.
