# Third Trimester (Weeks 28-40+)

## Medical checkpoints
- Visit cadence increases: every 2 weeks (wk 28-36), then weekly (wk 36+).
- Tdap vaccine (wk 27-36): given every pregnancy regardless of prior history; earlier in the window maximizes antibody transfer to baby.
- RSV vaccine (Abrysvo, wk 32-36): seasonal, given Sept-Jan in most of the US. Alternative is giving the baby the RSV antibody (nirsevimab) after birth; most babies do not need both.
- Group B strep swab (~wk 36-37): determines if antibiotics are needed during labor.
- Position check (~wk 36): breech triggers a C-section or version (ECV) discussion.
- Ongoing preeclampsia monitoring: blood pressure, urine protein, swelling.
- Possible non-stress tests if higher-risk or past due date.

## What's happening (mom)
- Heavier, more fatigue, back pain, swelling, heartburn, frequent urination.
- Braxton Hicks (practice contractions); nesting urge.
- Baby may "drop" (lightening) near the end.

## Do
- Pack hospital bag by ~wk 35-36 (see 04-hospital-go-bag).
- Install car seat ~wk 35+ and get it inspected (many hospitals/fire stations check).
- Finalize birth plan and review it with provider.
- Pre-register at the hospital.
- Wash baby clothes, set up nursery, stock freezer meals.
- Learn labor signs and the "when to call/go" thresholds (see 05).
- Do daily kick counts if instructed.

## Avoid (and warning signs to act on)
- All prior food/substance rules still apply.
- Long travel late (many airlines cut off ~wk 36; confirm with provider).
- Lying flat on back, heavy lifting, fall/ladder risk.
- CALL PROVIDER IMMEDIATELY for: severe headache, vision changes, sudden swelling, upper-right belly pain (preeclampsia signs), any bleeding, fluid leak, or reduced fetal movement.

## Partner (dad) tasks
- Lock in the hospital route + backup, parking, after-hours entrance, rideshare plan.
- Install and get the car seat inspected.
- Pack your own bag (snacks, charger, ID, cash, change of clothes).
- Be the contraction timer; know the go-threshold cold.
- Know the birth plan and be ready to advocate.
- Set the call tree: who to notify, pet/childcare, work coverage.
